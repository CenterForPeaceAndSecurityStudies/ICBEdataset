Reports from mid October 1962 confirmed that the Soviet Union was installing intermediate-range nuclear missiles in Cuba, ninety miles off the shore of Florida.
McNamara recalls this pivotal moment in the Cuban missile crisis and reads from the first of two letters that Soviet general secretary Nikita Khrushchev sent President John F Kennedy.
The period from November 1962 to the end of the Cuban missile crisis is generally seen as the most dangerous period of the entire Cold War.
