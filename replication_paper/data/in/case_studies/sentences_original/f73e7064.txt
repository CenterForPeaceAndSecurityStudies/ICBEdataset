In the interview he discusses the creation of the Nuclear Test Ban Treaty of 1963.
He goes on to describe the creation and some of the inner workings of the United States delegation to Moscow led by Averell Harriman that negotiated the Nuclear Test Ban Treaty.
He paints a vivid picture of Kennedys and Khrushchevs separate reactions to the success and of the dinner Khrushchev later gave at the Kremlin to celebrate the treaty, and notes that the treaty was a high point for both Khrushchev and Kennedys political careers.
He closes by describing what he believes are the right and wrong lessons of the missile crisis.
