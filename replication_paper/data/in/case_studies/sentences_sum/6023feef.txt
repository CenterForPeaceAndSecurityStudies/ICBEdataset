 These handsome white hardbacks with the really striking black and red cover graphics are easy on the eyes and first-rate mental nourishment for fact seekers .
 The latest one, on Colombia, caught my eye and made me dig around a little for some background on the rest of the rest .
 Since 1988, the Federal Research Division of the Library of Congress has prepared these excellent books . The books are published by the library of Congress .
 Country Studies has been around for more than 30 years - first as Area Handbooks and then Country Studies . Country Studies is the latest edition of the popular Country Studies series . The series has been published more than 40 years, with more than 200 editions .
 Not every nation is included in the U.S. list . The list was originally intended to focus on lesser-known areas of the world or regions in which United States might be deployed .
 The Colombia volume presents a concise history of the country . It also includes sections on geography, population, religions, education, and social movements .
 Economic structure, transportation and communications, financial regulations and markets, government and politics, the military and national security – you name it, and the subject is covered, and covered well .
 Of particular interest are a brief section on Illegal Drugs and a historical and political overview of social violence and the development of insurgencies in modern Colombia . The book also includes a brief overview of the history of the Colombian insurgency .
 I can’t think of a better serious introduction to the problems and prospects of this key Latin American country than this book . The book is published by Simon Tisdall .
 Most of the Country Studies series done in the past 25 years are available online only . Print copies of Colombia, North Korea, Iran and Cuba are still available .
 You can find these and other Country Studies in libraries and via various bricks and mortar and online used book outlets .
